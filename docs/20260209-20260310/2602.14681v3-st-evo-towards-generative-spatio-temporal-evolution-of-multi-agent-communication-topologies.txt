## ST-EVO: Towards Generative Spatio-Temporal Evolution of Multi-Agent Communication Topologies 

Xingjian Wu, Xvyuan Liu, Junkai Lu, Siyuan Wang, Xiangfei Qiu, Yang Shu , Jilin Hu , Chenjuan Guo , Bin Yang B

East China Normal University {xjwu, xvyuanliu, jklu, sywang, xfqiu}@stu.ecnu.edu.cn, {yshu, jlhu, cjguo, byang}@dase.ecnu.edu.cn 

Abstract 

LLM-powered Multi-Agent Systems (MAS) have emerged as an effective approach towards collaborative intelligence, and have attracted wide research interests. Among them, “self-evolving” MAS, treated as a more flexible and powerful technical route, can construct task-adaptive workflows or communication topolo-gies, instead of relying on a predefined static structue template. Current self-evolving MAS mainly focus on Spatial Evolving or Tempo-ral Evolving paradigm, which only considers the single dimension of evolution and does not fully incentivize LLMs’ collaborative capabil-ity. In this work, we start from a novel Spatio-Temporal perspective by proposing ST-EVO ,which supports dialogue-wise communication scheduling with a compact yet powerful flow-matching based Scheduler. To make precise Spatio-Temporal scheduling, ST-EVO can also perceive the uncertainty of MAS, and possesses self-feedback ability to learn from accumu-lated experience. Extensive experiments on nine benchmarks demonstrate the state-of-the-art performance of ST-EVO , achieving about 5%–25% accuracy improvement.

Evolving” and “Temporal Evolving”. Consider-ing the Spatial Evolving MAS, the mainstream approach is to first formulate the communication topology as a graph, then learn from some cases to train a “designer” (often compact neural networks), which can design topologies for specific tasks (Liu et al., 2023; Zhuge et al., 2024) or queries (Zhang et al.; Li et al., 2025a; Zhang et al., 2025b). How-ever, the designed topologies are fixed throughout the entire conversation process for a query. That means, if a complex query needs multiple dialogue iterations to solve, the communication topology cannot be dynamically tuned during iterations to allocate computing resources and functions among different agent nodes, which leads to low flexibility and robustness, and may cause high token con-sumption. On the other hand, though the Temporal Evolving MAS makes temporal split on workflows, most of them focus on routing single agent in the proper position (Niu et al., 2025; Zhang et al., 2024; Lee et al., 2025), which neglects the potential of collaboration in key temporal iterations. 

The questions in single-dimension evolution posed above can be explicitly summarized as: ❶

Performance. How to fully unleash the collab-orative capabilities of MAS in different dialogue iterations? ❷ Flexibility. How to make elastic tem-poral scheduling to fulfill the changing demand? 

❸ Efficiency. How to ensure efficiency consider-ing the token consumption of MAS and the cost of scheduling? To tackle these problems, we pro-pose ST-EVO , the first MAS with the capability of Spatio-Temporal Evolution, achieving dialogue-wise temporal scheduling for spatial communica-tion topologies. The motivation of ST-EVO is to precisely sched-ule multi-agent collaborations from both temporal and spatial dimensions, preserving performance, flexibility, and efficiency. To achieve this, we be-lieve the core elements lie in: ♣ Appropriate data structures. The structure should be able to well maintain the Spatio-Temporal trajectories, provid-ing the basis for scheduling. ♠ A compact but effective scheduler. The scheduling is frequent, and the demand for accuracy is high, thus calling for a lightweight and powerful scheduler. ♦ System state perception. The scheduler should consider the system state, such as whether it is reliable or not, thus leading to correct scheduling scheme. ♥

Self-feedback ability. The system needs to accu-mulate experience and learn underlying scheduling principles from it. Based on above-mentioned design principles, 

ST-EVO adopts Spatio-Temporal Graph to maintain the scheduling trajectories, which is an important structure in Spatio-Temporal data mining (Wang et al., 2020; Yang et al., 2024), able to characterize the spatial correlations (agent collaboration) at each temporal index (dialogue iteration). Considering the scheduler, we meticulously devise a compact neural network, driven by an MLP-based Flow-Matching (Lipman et al., 2023) module, which pos-sesses strong generative mechanism while keeping lightweight, and can work as a stochastic inter-polant (Wu et al., 2026a) to directly evolve the communication topology temporally. Additionally, to perceive the system state and provide self-feedback ability, we devise mecha-nisms driven by both entropy and experience, then internalize them through preference optimization. First, we supervise the system state with the help of 

Entropy Distribution to evaluate the uncertainty of the MAS, and we internalize it into the scheduler via reinforcement learning. Second, considering the self-feedback ability, we draw inspiration from 

Experience Learning (Zhang et al., 2025a; Tang et al., 2025), and construct a retrieve-augment mod-ule to accumulate successful scheduling trajectories as experience. During training, we also internal-ize the experience, which retrieves corresponding historical scheduling trajectories as reference, and regularizes the current scheduling scheme. Our Contributions can be summarized as: 

❶ We introduce ST-EVO , pioneering the explo-ration of Spatio-Temporal Evolving MAS. It can support dialogue-wise real-time schedul-ing through generative Spatio-Temporal evo-lution of communication topologies. 

❷ We devise a compact generative scheduler driven by Flow-Matching. It can learn under-lying scheduling principles from entropy dis-tribution and historical experience, thus pos-sessing strong query-aware and dialogue-wise scheduling capability. 

❸ Comprehensive experiments on 9 benchmarks from multiple real-world tasks demonstrate the state-of-the-art performance of our pro-posed ST-EVO .

2 Related Works 

2.1 LLM-powered Multi-Agent Systems 

Recent studies introduce a surge of multi-agent sys-tems to incentivize LLMs’ swarm intelligence in complex scenarios. The communication topology occupies a central position in them and includes multiple structures. Among them, non-interactive systems organize agents parallelly without explicit communication, such as Mixture-of-Agents (Wang et al., 2025d) and LLM-Debate (Estornell and Liu, 2024); Chain MAS arranges agents sequentially as a workflow, such as MetaGPT (Hong et al., 2023) and ChatDev (Qian et al., 2024); Tree MAS works in a task-splitting mode with a chief node and hierarchical child nodes (Yao et al., 2023); Star MAS works in a task-assignment mode with a cen-tral node and multiple edge nodes, seen in Auto-Gen (Wu et al.) and MiniGrid (Zhou et al., 2024); Graph MAS, as the most general form, can repre-sent all of above-posed topologies and are widely applied (Inc., 2024; Zhuge et al., 2024; Wu et al.). 

2.2 Self-evolving Multi-Agent Systems 

To improve efficiency, recent research interests shift towards “self-evolving” MAS (Wang et al., 2025e; Fang et al., 2025), which can contribute to fully-automated MAS customization for changing tasks. These studies characterize multi-agent com-munication topologies as graphs (Liu et al., 2023; Zhuge et al., 2024; Zhang et al.) to organize and represent relationships between agents. Compared with conventional static communication topologies, they support task-aware (Liu et al., 2023; Zhuge et al., 2024) or even query-aware (Zhang et al.; Li et al., 2025a; Zhang et al., 2025b) dynamic com-munication topology customization. We categorize them into Spatial Evolving and Temporal Evolv-ing MAS. For Spatial Evolving MAS, they mainly focus on the performance, robustness, efficiency, and redundancy (Zhang et al., 2025b) when cus-tomizing the topology. However, they pay little attention to temporal scheduling which is crucial in MAS with dialogue-wise varying resource de-mands. For Temporal Evolving MAS (Zhang et al., 2024; Niu et al., 2025; Lee et al., 2025), though they consider the temporal scheduling in different iterations, most of them neglect utilizing the col-laborative capability of MAS inside each iteration. In this work, we propose ST-EVO , the first Spatio-Temporal Evolving MAS, which supports temporal scheduling for spatial communication topologies.

4 ST-EVO 

As illustrated in Figure 2, our proposed ST-EVO 

builds multi-agent systems from a spatio-temporal perspective. (1) ST-EVO supports query-aware MAS construction, which builds agents with pro-vided LLMs, profiles and Tools, and generates the initial communication topology based on the query. (2) ST-EVO supports spatio-temporal scheduling, where a generative Scheduler is devised to evolve the dialogue-wise spatial communication topolo-gies. (3) The Scheduler is driven by entropy and experience to perceive the system states and learn from accumulated experience. (4) The Scheduler is optimized on dozens of samples to internalize the state-aware capability and experience, thus sup-porting precise spatio-temporal scheduling.

4.2 Generative Scheduler 

As introduced in Section 3, given an initial an-chor topology G0, the Spatio-Temporal Scheduling is to autoregressively plan the future topologies 

[G1, G2, · · · , GT ] for dialogue iterations. And it needs to simultaneously ensure ❶ peformance, ❷

flexibility, and ❸ efficiency. Intuitively, adopting generative mechanisms can ensure the diversity and quality of the scheduling trajectories, and avoid overfitting during training. However, most generative mechanisms like DDPM, GAN, and VAE cannot balance these three aspects. To tackle this, since the scheduling task in ST-EVO 

is Spatio-Temporal Graph Generation, we draw inspiration from spatio-temporal data mining, for which lightweight flow-matching networks (Liu et al., 2025b; Wu et al., 2026a) are competent. Specifically, we devise a compact flow-matching based Scheduler to ensure the ❶ performance and 

❸ efficiency. For ❷ flexibility, we utilize an im-portant property of flow-matching to serve as a stochastic interpolant, which can start from a given point, instead of a random Gaussian noise, bridg-ing the transport path between two arbitrary dis-tributions (Wu et al., 2026a). Its core architecture consists of a GCN (Zhang et al., 2019) and an MLP-based flow-matching network (FM-Net). The FM-Net utilizes the AdaLN (Peebles and Xie, 2023) to integrate the condition Ht. 6 Conclusion 

In this work, we introduce ST-EVO , the first Spatio-Temporal Evolving MAS, which supports query-aware and dialogue-wise communication topol-ogy scheduling. Comprehensive experiments on 9 benchmarks demonstrate the state-of-the-art perfor-mance of ST-EVO , and it can ensure both efficiency and robustness on complex scenarios. Limitations 

While ST-EVO demonstrates advancement on mul-tiple tasks, it has limitations. Since it follows the few-shot learning paradigm, the Scheduler needs to be trained on a few corresponding samples to rec-ognize the task characteristics. However, in some real-world scenarios with scarce data, this mecha-nism may lose its advantages. In the future, we aim at building a fully zero-shot and effective mech-anism to support construction and scheduling for MAS. 

Ethics Statement 

As for ethical considerations, our study only em-ploys standard public datasets and commonly used open-source frameworks, and we find no obvious ethical issues in this work.

D.1 Time-Series and Spatio-Temporal Data Analysis 

The analysis of time-series data is a fundamen-tal challenge in numerous fields, with recent re-search increasingly shifting from classical statis-tical models to deep learning-based solutions. A comprehensive survey of these methods from a channel strategy perspective highlights the diver-sity of approaches for multivariate forecasting (Qiu et al., 2025a). A primary focus in this area is the development of robust models for long-sequence time-series forecasting. Innovations in training paradigms and model architecture, such as the use of a quadratic direct forecast approach (Wang et al., 2026a) and the exploration of selective represen-tation spaces from a patch perspective (Wu et al., 2025a), have been proposed to enhance prediction accuracy. The move towards generative approaches is also notable, with models aiming for univer-sal multimodal forecasting capabilities (Wu et al., 2026a) or employing frameworks like Koopman-Kalman enhanced Variational AutoEncoders for probabilistic forecasting (Wu et al., 2025b). Concurrently, significant attention has been given to the design of objective functions to bet-ter guide the learning process. Methodologies such as learning to forecast in the frequency do-main (Wang et al., 2025b), employing transformed label alignment (Wang et al., 2025c), and using decomposition-based loss functions (Qiu et al., 2025d) have proven effective. Other work focuses on improving the alignment between the model’s output and the ground truth, for instance, by mini-mizing the Wasserstein distance between their joint distributions (Wang et al., 2026b). The field also addresses specific complexities inherent in time-series data. For multivariate se-ries, methods like dual clustering have been intro-duced to enhance forecasting (Qiu et al., 2025e), while other approaches leverage dual causal net-works to effectively incorporate exogenous vari-ables (Qiu et al., 2025f). For irregular time series, there has been a call to rethink complex models in favor of simple yet effective baselines (Liu et al., 2025a). Beyond forecasting, related tasks such as time-series imputation have also benefited from advanced techniques like optimal transport (Wang et al., 2025a). The parallel task of anomaly detec-tion is also evolving, with methods using channel-aware frequency patching (Wu et al., 2025c). Given the rapid proliferation of new methods, the community has emphasized the need for automa-tion, standardized benchmarking, and accessible tools. Efforts towards zero-shot joint neural archi-tecture and hyperparameter search (Wu et al., 2024) and fully automated forecasting systems (Wu et al., 2025d) aim to streamline the modeling pipeline. To ensure rigorous evaluation, comprehensive and fair benchmarking frameworks have been devel-oped for both forecasting (Qiu et al., 2024) and anomaly detection (Qiu et al., 2025c). Further-more, the development of accessible toolkits aims to make time series forecasting easy for a broader audience (Qiu et al., 2025b). The practical impact of these sophisticated frameworks is demonstrated in their application to critical real-world problems, including the development of accurate and inter-pretable systems for ensuring industrial security (Wang et al., 2023a). 

D.2 Controllable Spatio-Temporal Content Generation 

The principles of modeling sequential data are also central to the generation and manipulation of high-dimensional spatio-temporal content like video. As detailed in a recent survey on the topic (Ma et al., 2025a), a major trend in this field is controllable video generation. Researchers have developed a va-riety of methods that allow for fine-grained control over the synthesized output using intuitive user in-puts. For example, video generation can be guided by pose information, enabling animation from a sequence of poses even when models are trained on pose-free videos (Ma et al., 2024a). Similarly, motion transfer can be achieved through efficient spatial-temporal decoupled finetuning (Ma et al., 2025d). The scope of control also extends to more direct interactions, such as animating a specific image region based on a simple click and motion prompt (Ma et al., 2025c) or creating expressive portrait animations guided by emojis (Ma et al., 2024b). Subsequent work has focused on mak-ing these animation processes more efficient (Ma et al., 2025e). This paradigm of control is also be-ing applied to video editing and content creation, with methods that use control handle transforma-tions for precise edits (Ma et al., 2023) and leverage video inpainting to empower 4D creation (Ma et al., 2025b).
