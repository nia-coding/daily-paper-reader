## 摘要
智能体技能（Agent Skills）是结构化的程序性知识包，旨在推理阶段增强大语言模型（LLM）智能体的能力。尽管已被广泛采用，但目前仍缺乏标准方法来衡量其是否真正有效。我们提出了 SkillsBench，这是一个包含 11 个领域、86 个任务的基准测试，并配备了精心策划的技能和确定性验证器。每个任务在三种条件下进行评估：无技能、策划技能和自生成技能。我们在 7,308 条轨迹上测试了 7 种智能体-模型配置。结果显示，策划技能使平均通过率提高了 16.2 个百分点（pp），但效果因领域而异（从软件工程的 +4.5pp 到医疗保健的 +51.9pp 不等），且 84 个任务中有 16 个出现了负增长。平均而言，自生成技能没有带来收益，这表明模型无法可靠地编写其自身能从中获益的程序性知识。此外，包含 2-3 个模块的针对性技能优于全面的文档，且配备技能的小型模型可以媲美不配备技能的大型模型。

## Abstract
Agent Skills are structured packages of procedural knowledge that augment LLM agents at inference time. Despite rapid adoption, there is no standard way to measure whether they actually help. We present SkillsBench, a benchmark of 86 tasks across 11 domains paired with curated Skills and deterministic verifiers. Each task is evaluated under three conditions: no Skills, curated Skills, and self-generated Skills. We test 7 agent-model configurations over 7,308 trajectories. Curated Skills raise average pass rate by 16.2 percentage points(pp), but effects vary widely by domain (+4.5pp for Software Engineering to +51.9pp for Healthcare) and 16 of 84 tasks show negative deltas. Self-generated Skills provide no benefit on average, showing that models cannot reliably author the procedural knowledge they benefit from consuming. Focused Skills with 2--3 modules outperform comprehensive documentation, and smaller models with Skills can match larger models without them.
